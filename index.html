01 / About

From circuits to economies

My path into economics began after roughly eight years working in electronics and communications engineering. That background shapes how I approach economic problems now: I look for the underlying mechanism, build models that can be tested and reproduced, and I'm comfortable moving between quantitative rigor and plain-language explanation.

I hold an M.S. in Economics from Purdue University, with additional economics training from the London School of Economics. Today I work as an Economic Consultant with NOAA's Office of National Marine Sanctuaries, where I apply benefit-cost analysis, IMPLAN regional impact modeling, NEPA analysis, and fisheries economics to questions about how marine sanctuaries generate economic and social value.

Alongside applied consulting work, I'm active in economics education and quantitative research methodology — including using LLM-assisted classification with formal validation (Cohen's kappa, versioned taxonomies) to make large-scale literature analysis reproducible.
